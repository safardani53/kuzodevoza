Dad's spectacular Christmas light display draws huge crowd for big switch on - and even Home Secreta

 Posted on March 20, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  982 words  |  Martina Birk

A BIG-HEARTED resident has transformed his front garden into a real life winter wonderland, raising thousands of pounds for charity with a Christmas light's switch-on which was even attended by Home Secretary Amber Rudd.

Paul Brockington, 52, and his wife Rosina have given their Hastings home a Christmas makeover complete with thousands of lights to drum up donations for the local St Michael's Hospice annually for the past five years.

The couple have lit up Helens Park Road with 20,000 twinkly lights, 600m of fake snow, a miniature railway, fake polar bears, penguins and reindeer and even a miniature MOUNTAIN complete with a snow machine.

They switched on the lights of Blacklands Winter Wonderland on Friday to a delighted crowd of over 1,500 people, including Home Secretary and local MP Amber Rudd.

The big switch-on included a fireworks display, and Frozen's Anna and Elsa were there to entertain kids.

The couple also had a miniature railway, the Santa Express, in the front garden and Santa peeping through the curtains to wave at excited families.

Paul told The Sun Online he's been amazed by the response he's had this year, which soared after a video of his display went viral on Facebook - hitting over ONE MILLION views.

He said: "We couldn’t believe it this year. Amber Rudd's office phoned us and asked us if we mind if she came down. She thought it was brilliant.

"It’s amazing. We’re getting crowds most nights now. A lot of people have seen the video and that’s why they’ve come over - I've been out there and talking to some of them and they said they said it’s better than on Facebook!"

Paul, who runs his own business, Blacklands Fire, with wife Rosina, says they're aiming to raise £5,000 for the hospice this year, which will make their total from the past five years an incredible £20,000.

And it all started with one of his neighbours complimenting him on the Christmas lights and decor outside their house five years ago.

He said: "We started off by putting a few lights outside one year, and a few neighbours aid it brightened up the area. So the next year I decided to just go for it, and when we turned the lights on there was about 150 people outside. It grew and grew each year.

"We've got the miniature railway, one of my friends brings the train up and runs the Santa Express up and down the driveway for the kids.

"Then we’ve got the pyrotechnics companies who do fireworks for us, with proper display shells so they explode above the house.

"We’ve also got a choir. The first year we had a choir with 8 people, this year 25!  Anna and Elsa from Frozen were here this year.

"We had hot dogs and burgers vans and to top it all there was at least at least 1,000, if not more, outside the house. It was completely mad!"

The couple spend months preparing, with Paul buying in new decorations every January and starting to put together the display in September.

He added: "Outside this year we’ve built a mountain on one side of the garden, and we’ve got in excess of 20,000 lights all together.

"The whole of the front garden is described into a winter wonderland. It’s covered in  snow, we’ve got two waterfalls of lights which come into a river, the into a pond and a lake.

"We’ve got reindeer, penguins, polar bears and seals, all light up ones. We've got a snow machine on the the top of the mountain with snow coming down as well.

"To top it all off we’ve got Santa walking about the bedroom window!"

The couple switch on their lights every night up until Christmas, and have 'special guests' every weekend for local kids - with Iggle Piggle from hit kids show In The Night Garden and Dora the Explorer popping by this Friday.

They've already raised £4,000 for St Michael's - only £1,000 off their £5,000 target. The charity is close to the couple's hearts, looking after Paul's granddad, Rosina's mum and other family friends.

Paul explained: "They need all the money they can get - it's not just the patients they look after, it’s the family as well. They help them through it all. Most of the money they need is by fundraising., they don’t get much money anywhere else."

And the lights are a real community affair, with neighbours donating costumes, decorations and even letting the couple use their driveway for stalls and electricity for extra lights.

Paul said: "The whole community round here, everybody seems to love it!" and he thanked wife Rosina for her hard work - which includes untangling and testing 20,000 lights.

He added: "The one person who is brilliant is my wife. She tests all the lights and unravels the wires. Without her I couldn’t do it."

But despite the popularity of the event the couple have decided this year will be the last - as they plan on swapping their Hastings winter wonderland for the real thing with a trip to Lapland or the German Christmas markets next year.

Paul added: "We can’t go away any time for a weekend because I’m out there every half an hour emptying the [money] box.

"We’re just tied to the house from when they go on to New Year's Eve. We’ve decided we’ve done five years so we’re going to go away next year to a German Christmas market or Lapland or something.

"But will all the pressure put on us we might have to make a comeback!"
