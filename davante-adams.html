Early Life & Education

Davante Lavell Adams shortly, Davante Adams was born in Redwood City, California, to his parents, Pamela Brown and Douglas Adams. Besides his parents, Adams has three siblings; two sisters and a brother.

While growing up, Adams had already thought about what he wanted to be in his life.

As a fourth-grader, he would write how he wanted to be an NBA or NFL; looking at him now, we can see that he is pretty successful in his career.

He attended Palo Alto High School, where he played football.

As a senior two-way starter for the Vikings football team, he led the team to a CIF state championship.

All in all, he had posted 64 receptions for 1,094 yards and 12 touchdowns. At the same time, Adams was also playing basketball and was a two-star recruit, with an average of 9.0 points, 5.8 rebounds, and 5.4 assists per game.

College

After high school graduation, Davante enrolled in the California State University, Fresno, where he played football for the Fresno State Bulldogs team.

Adams commenced as a redshirted freshman, only playing a season under head coach Pat Hill. Later, they had Tim DeRuyter as their new head coach.

He had his debut match on September 1, 2012, against the Weber State Wildcats.

They won the match as Davante had 118 receiving yards and two touchdowns. Following it, by October, Adams had eight consecutive games with a receiving touchdown.

He claimed the conference’s Freshman of the Year and was an All-American freshman. Besides, Davante also became the MVP of the 2012 Hawaii Bowl.

During the following season, Adams commenced with the first match against Rutgers Scarlet Knights, which came as a victory.

He ended the season with a match against the USC Trojans in the 2013 Las Vegas Bowl, where he had recorded 168 yards and a touchdown.

Altogether, Davante had maintained 131 receptions for 1,719 yards and 24 touchdowns.

As for his college days, Adams had 233 receptions and 38 touchdown receptions while he played two seasons for the Bulldogs.

By the end, The Associated Press had named him second-team All-America while he set a Mountain West Conference record with his 24 touchdown receptions.

After it, Adams let go of his remaining senior college years to enter the 2014 NFL Draft.

Professional Career

Green Bay Packers

During the 2014 NFL Draft, the Green Bay Packers selected Adams in the second round as the 53rd overall pick. They signed a contract on June 12, and Adams became the 9th wide receiver to be fixed.

Adams debuted in the number four receiver slot against Seattle Seahawks. 

During his tenure, Adams was the Packers’ number three receiver who had his first NFL touchdown through Aaron Rodgers against the Minnesota Vikings.

Overall, for his rookie year, Adams had recorded 446 receiving yards and three touchdowns. Additionally, he had the rookie record for receiving yards during the playoff game over the Dallas Cowboys.

Davante struggled with a knee injury the following season, which kept him off through a few games.

All in all, he maintained seven receptions for a season-high 93 yards against Carolina Panthers.

By the end of the season, Adams took the “off-season MVP” award as he had 50 receptions, 483 receiving yards, and a touchdown.

Although Adams had a knee injury, he appeared until the season’s end game. Thus, he maintained 111 receptions and 133 yards.
