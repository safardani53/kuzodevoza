DWTS fans in tears after Selma Blairs emotional comeback performance &amp; say she should have won

 Posted on July 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  483 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

DANCING With the Stars fans have been left in tears after Selma Blair returned to the ballroom on finale night.

After she gave an emotional performance, many fans thought the actress could have won the whole competition, if she had stayed.

Selma shocked everyone when she joined DWTS, after suffering from Multiple Sclerosis.

The 50-year-old was first diagnosed with MS in 2018.

She was partnered with Sasha Farber, who has never won the competition.

Despite impressing the judges week after week, she had to withdraw from season 31, due to doctor's orders.

On Monday night's finale, however, Selma returned, along with the other eliminated contestants, for one last performance.

As fellow contestant Jordin Sparks sang a live rendition of her song, No Air, she and Sasha danced contemporary to the song.

The ballroom was left in tears and Twitter erupted with emotion as well.

"Selma won In my heart," wrote one fan.

"Selma dances with so much grace and elegance. I have no doubt she would be participating in this finale had her health permitted" said another.

A third added: "Selma would of won that mirror ball," while one user chimed in, "Selma won this season."

An emotional fan asked: "Can someone please hand the mirror ball trophy to Selma Blair already? That was absolutely lovely. I'm not crying you're crying."

"Definitely think had Selma been able to continue, she would have won. She’s the real winner of this season for so many reasons even beyond dance!" one commenter said.

SAD TWIST

Week four saw Selma leaving the competition, and she left the entire ballroom in tears with one last emotional dance.

The judges gave her her first perfect score.

In a rehearsal video that played before her heart-wrenching live performance, the Cruel Intentions star sat down with dancing partner Sasha.

"I had these MRIs, and the results came back, and it all adds up to - I can't go on with the competition," she told him, holding back tears.

"With a chronic illness, you do have special consideration, and my body is definitely taking a hit," she explained.

Selma continued: "It's just way too much for the safety of my bones. There's just intensive bone trauma and inflammation among rips and tears, so I could do extensive damage that, of course, I do not want."

As she took to the ballroom, the star had tears in her eyes while her co-stars including Jordin, Gabby Windey, Shangela, Charli D'Amelio, and Jessie James Decker were all in tears.

Pros Gleb Savchenko, Emma Slater, and Alan Bersten were all seen crying hysterically too.
