Julian Champagnie’s Family!

Julian Champagnie was born on June 29, 2001, in Staten Island, New York, to Christina and Ranford Champagnie.

He has an elder twin brother Justin, and a younger brother Jaylen.

His father, Ranford, was the head coach of the soccer team of Baruch College from 2003 to 2017. 

The Bearcats appointed Ranford as the men’s soccer coach on December 15, 2003. Before joining as the soccer coach, he was the access supervisor at Verizon.

Ranford attended high school at Campion College in Jamaica, West Indies. He then finished college at St. John’s University in 1999 and majored in marketing research.

During his time in college, Ranford also played soccer and led the team to the 1996 NCAA Division I National Championship.

Ranford hoped that one of his twin sons would follow his path, but things went differently than he expected, as both are currently basketball players. His youngest son, Jaylen, has also been already hooked on basketball.

Her mother, Christina, is a school teacher at the St. Peter Catholic Academy.

She received her degree from St. Francis College and has taught in the Catholic School System for over 21 years.

His elder twin brother Justin plays for the Sioux Falls Skyforce in the NBA G League.

High School And Collegiate Basketball

Julian attended Bishop Loughlin Memorial High School and played basketball alongside his twin brother.

He averaged 15 points per game in his junior year and 17.2 points per game in his senior year.

Julian joined St. John’s over Pittsburgh, Washington State, Cincinnati, and Seton Hall offers. However, his twin brother decided to play collegiate basketball for Pittsburgh.

Throughout the history of basketball, many pairs of talented twins have played college basketball.

Examples include Markieff and Marcus Morris from Kansas, Aaron and Andrew Harrison from Kentucky, Caleb and Cody Martin from NC  and Nevada, as well as Brook and Robin Lopez and Jason and Jarron Collins from Stanford.

Julian finished his college career as the 20th-highest scorer with 1408 points and fourth on the career 3-pointers with 149.

Professional Career 

After going undrafted in the 2022 NBA Draft, Julian signed a two-way contract with the Philadelphia Sixers.

The 2021 Big East Co-Most Improved Player made his NBA debut for the Philadelphia 76ers against the Brooklyn Nets on November 22, 2022, in which he featured for two minutes.

He only played two games for the Philadelphia 76ers in the NBA and thirty-two games for the Delaware Blue Coats in the NBA G League.

Julian made his G League career-high of 39 points against the Capital City Go-Go on October 10, 2022.

On February 14, 2023, the Sixers ended their contract with Julian & after two days, the San Antonio Spurs claimed the waiver.

Battle Of Twin Brothers: Julian And Justin

Julian was born seven minutes after Justin, and they both began playing basketball at the age of 5.

While playing, Justin was energetic and expressive, often showing his aggression and trash-talking, whereas Julian was the quieter of the two.

Julian entered the NBA draft in 2022 as a junior, while his twin brother Justin entered a year earlier in 2021 as a sophomore, but unfortunately, both were not selected.

On December 1, 2022, the twins went head to head in a game between the Delaware Blue Coats (Julian’s team) and the Raptors 905 (Justin’s team) in the NBA G League.

The younger twin, Julian, emerged victorious with a 127-117 win over his older brother’s team.

In the match, both brothers recorded a double-double, with Julian scoring 27 points and grabbing ten rebounds and Justin scoring 22 points and getting ten rebounds.
