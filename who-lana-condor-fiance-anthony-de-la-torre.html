ON Friday, actress Lana Condor announced her engagement to her boyfriend, actor and musician Anthony De La Torre, in an Instagram post.

The couple has been together for over six years.

Who is Lana Condor's fiancé Anthony De La Torre?

Born November 24, 1993, Anthony De La Torre is an American actor, singer, songwriter, and guitarist.

De La Torre was born to Cuban parents in Bowling Green, Ohio. 

Esther García, his mother, is a Spanish teacher at Bowling Green High School who also runs a cleaning company. 

De La Torre's parents split shortly after he was born. 

He has two siblings: Nicolas, his older brother, and Angelica, his older sister.

What has Anthony De La Torre been in?

De La Torre began his acting career as Willie the Whistling Duck in Go, Diego, Go!, for which he was credited as Antonio Cabrera. 

He was also featured in a print commercial for Del Monte ketchup. 

He had a recurring part as a Latino actor called Anthony Del Rey in two episodes of Nickelodeon's 100 Things to Do Before High School. 

De La Torre featured as a young Jack Sparrow in Joachim Rønning and Espen Sandberg's blockbuster picture Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales, which was released in 2017. 

CGI was used to combine his and Johnny Depp's appearances as the character. 

In July 2017, he took over as frontman for Mike Krompass, Randy Cooke, and Billy Sheehan's rock band The Fell, replacing Toby Rand.

What did Lana Condor say about her engagement to Anthony De La Torre?

In an Instagram post made on January 28, 2022, Condor announced her engagement to De La Torre.

The post, which highlighted the happy couple and her engagement ring, was captioned: “Saying YES was the easiest decision I’ve ever made. 

“I consider myself the absolute luckiest woman alive to live in your sphere. 

“Aside from my father, without a doubt, you are the greatest man in the world. 

“Emmy & Timmy [the couple’s dogs] said it’s about time mommy & daddy got engaged,” Condor wrote.

She also shared information about where De La Torre had the ring designed, Paris Jewellers, which is a female-owned, Vietnamese business.
