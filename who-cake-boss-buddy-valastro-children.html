Who is Cake Boss star Buddy Valastro's wife Lisa?

 Posted on August 27, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  633 words  |  Larita Shotwell

REALITY television star Lisa Valastro is a podcast co-host and the wife of Cake Boss Buddy Valastro.

Buddy and Lisa have been married for nearly two decades and share four children.

Who is Lisa Valastro?

Lisa Valastro is the wife of Cake Boss star Buddy Valastro and the co-host of the What's Up Cuz? podcast.

The couple were married in 2001 and have four kids - Sofia, Bartolo Buddy Jr, Marco, and Carlo.

Lisa is prominently featured on Cake Boss, which has been on the air since 2009.

Her husband owns Carlo's Bakery in Hoboken, New Jersey, and is the face of Buddy V's Ristorante.

Additionally, Lisa is the co-host of the What's Up Cuz? podcast with Erica Spera.

They "sit down to share family stories from behind the scenes of the Cake Boss family," according to the podcast description.

"Join us like it's Sunday dinner as we answer fan emails about life, love, and of course family. 

"Don't miss out on what happens when the cameras stop rolling."

A new podcast appears every Monday.

Who are the Valastro kids?

Daughter Sofia is 18 years old, son Bartolo "Buddy" Valastro III is 17 years old, Marco is 14 and youngest son Carlo is 10 years old.

All four children have been known to make cameos on Cake Boss, and have some presence on social media.

"I am a busy guy. As busy as I am I still have a strong bond and relationship with my kids," Buddy told lifeofdad.com in an exclusive interview in 2016.

"I am not bragging, but I have done a lot of good stuff, but my biggest accomplishment is my kids and my family,” he added.

What happened to Buddy?

In September 2020, Buddy Valastro revealed he was hospitalized after a "horrific" bowling accident that "required multiple surgeries" on his hand.

Lisa Valastro sobbed hysterically in the 911 call following the accident.

The chef's representative spoke to People about the accident and revealed that it happened while the chef was spending "quality time" with his family at their New Jersey home, which has its own bowling alley.

The rep said: "There was a malfunction with the bowling pinsetter, a common fix in the past, but it turned into a terrible accident.

"After trying to release the bowling pin from the cage mechanism, his right hand became lodged and compressed inside the unit.

"Unable to remove his hand, he can see a 1-1/2 metal rod slowly and repeatedly impale his hand three times between his ring finger and middle finger."

Buddy Jr and Marco then used a reciprocating saw "to cut through the metal rod and relieve his father from the machine."

How is Buddy doing one year after the accident?

Buddy has had a total of five surgeries since the accident, and he joked during an interview on The Today Show on October 5, that although he's on the mend, he'll "probably never be a hand model."

In February 2021, Buddy said he had graduated to making a fist, and his dominant right hand that was impaled is about 95 percent back to normal.
