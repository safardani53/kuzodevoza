CANDY fans are begging an iconic brand to bring back a limited-time item that they say was one of the best.

People want to once again taste the nostalgic flavor of Skittles bubble gum that had a spot on grocery store shelves in the 90s and early 2000s.

In various corners of the internet, young adults can be found wistfully discussing old snacks that disappeared throughout the years.

One treat that continues to get attention on Twitter and elsewhere is Skittles bubble gum.

The gum comes in a memorable rectangular package that holds a handful of colorful gum pieces.

The candy appears to be just the normal sweet at first, but fans will remember the Skittle would crush into a gum that was delicious, yet fleeting.

"Skittles gum used to be the best 20 seconds of my day as a youngin," one Twitter user joked.

Despite the teasing, many people really do want the candy to make a triumphant return and wonder why it was taken off the shelves.

"I want some skittles gum so bad," one person tweeted last week.

Another user asked her followers if they remembered the treat, saying that she used to tear them up.

"Bring back Skittles gum, why did y'all take that away from us," a different devastated user asked.

Parent company Mars Wrigley has yet to address the call for change since discontinuing the product in 2006.

The U.S. Sun has reached out to the company to see if the product will return sometime soon.

This latest cry for a candy's return comes as Hershey's silenced begging fans who begged the brand to bring back Reese's Swoops.

Reese’s Swoops have not been seen since 2006 when they were discontinued after three years in stores.

Nostalgic chocolate fans kept nudging Hershey’s for years, inquiring whether the treat will be making a return.

It does not appear that a Swoops comeback will be imminent.

A Hershey's spokesperson told The U.S. Sun: "It’s always great to see the love and enthusiasm for our products, but at this time there are no immediate plans for Swoops to return."

Oreo has also confirmed that one of their so-called best products has been taken off shelves in what fans say was an uncalled-for move.
