Four discontinued childhood snacks we loved as adults and we'll never see them again

 Posted on May 28, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  532 words  |  Martina Birk

WHEN your childhood snacks get discontinued, it may hurt harder.

Over the years, sweet eaters have seen their favorites come and go as companies tend to discontinue products due to low sales or little to no demand.

Fans have been missing items like Wonka Bars, Marathon Bars, Butterfinger BBs, and more for years.

Below, we rounded up four snacks that fans have had to say goodbye to.

However, you may be able to find many of these treats on eBay, Amazon, or specialty stores.

1. DUNKAROOS

You may remember this snack if you were a kid in the 1990s as it was in many lunchboxes around the nation.

Dunkaroos were a pack of cookies on one side and frosting to dip them in on the other.

Shoppers could choose from different shapes like kangaroos with hats on, hot air balloons, motorcycles, capital D’s, and more.

Plus, each packaging had other icing flavors to accompany the different cookie shapes.

This included the infamous Rainbow Sprinkle, Chocolate Chip, Cinnamon, and Peanut Butter.

The turquoise packaging made them instantly recognizable and enviable for those who didn’t see them in their lunches.

While school ended for adults, so did the fan-favorite treat back in 2012 as the brand wanted to focus on more healthy products, according to Yahoo!.

2. KEEBLER MAGIC MIDDLES

In a blink of an eye, these flavor-filled cookies left shelves to the disappointment of many.

The shortbread cookies came packed with either fudge or peanut butter filling and disappeared with little to no explanation.

While they’ve been gone for over a decade, sweet eaters started a Facebook page with thousands of members called BRING BACK Keebler MAGIC MIDDLE COOKIES!!!

Within the page, users share stories about the beloved treat and even have started a petition.

The page may be loud, but the cookie maker Keebler has been quiet.

Back in 2011, members posted on the Facebook page that while other companies have hopped on the filling trend, they didn’t compare.

One user wrote: “Has anyone noticed that a lot of companies are coming out with filled cookies???? Chips Ahoy now has one, Target, and Pepperidge Farms! Come on Keebler! Get with it!!!!!”

3. DANNON SPRINKL'INS

A treat that was once favored in the 1990s, has since left shelves back in the 2000s.

The Dannon Sprinkl'ins was yogurt that came with sprinkles attached to the top of the package to add some fun.

While the snack was popular with kids around the US, it quietly disappeared leaving many fans wanting more.

One fan wrote on Twitter: “Dannon please bring back Sprinkl’ins. Thank you.” 

Another wrote: “Ever since Dannon Sprinkl'ins yogurt was discontinued I've felt a hole in my heart.”

4. JELL-O PUDDING POPS

These pudding pops were a nice summer treat after a long school day that came in chocolate and vanilla.

However, after years of low sales, the company decided to pull them from store shelves.

While other snack makers tried to emulate the fan favorite, it was never up to par with customers.
