Jack in the Box fans say their hearts are crushed as fast food chain removes beloved menu item

 Posted on February 11, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  358 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

JACK in the Box fans are devastated because a popular menu item has been discontinued after nine years. 

Customers have even started an online position to bring it back.

Jack in the Box has quietly discontinued the grande sausage burrito, sparking online outrage among customers.

First introduced to the menu in 2014, the entrée featured sausage, hash browns, bacon crumbles, shredded pepper jack, cheddar cheese sauce, and creamy siracha, wrapped in a warm tortilla.

But customers began noticing last month that it has been removed from the menus at all 2,200 Jack in the Box restaurants.

Now, a petition to “revive the grande sausage burrito” on change.org has received 244 signatures as of September 18.

‘HEARTS CRUSHED’

“With our hearts crushed, destroyed, no, obliterated, we humbly ask all of you, the wonderful people of this, our great country America, to sign this petition,” wrote the campaigner on the petition’s website.

“This is hands down my favorite breakfast burrito,” wrote one supporter.

“Why Jack?” they asked.

Others have taken to Reddit to express their disbelief over the menu removal.

“It was my go-to burrito; one of my absolute favorites and it’s gone,” wrote one Redditor in disbelief.

“It was my hubby’s favorite too! We were heartbroken,” posted another.

Jack in the Box customers looking for a breakfast burrito now have just one option - the meat lovers’ breakfast burrito with bacon, sausage, and ham.

But this entrée is missing the spicy sriracha sauce that made the grande sausage burrito so beloved.

RING THE BELL

Taco Bell is another fast food destination popular for its burritos.

It recently brought back a previously discontinued burrito following a fan vote.

Taco Bell gave fans the opportunity to pick their favorite - either the beefy crunch burrito or the cool ranch Doritos locos taco.

The beefy crunch burrito emerged victorious with 60 percent of the vote and was reinstated on the Taco Bell menu.
