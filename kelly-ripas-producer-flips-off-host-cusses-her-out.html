Kelly Ripa's producing partner Albert Bianchini has cussed out and flipped off the Live host as she drops to the floor in a wild behind-the-scenes video.

The Live with Kelly and Mark host posted the unscripted video on her Instagram account on Monday.

Captioned: "#MeditationMonday minus the meditation. #LetsTalkOffCamera BTS @wiener_jan @abtvhd," the post showed Kelly, 52, and television producers Albert and Jan Schillay Wiener attempting—and failing—to meditate.

Albert appeared keen to commit to the exercise as he played the guided meditation from his smartphone.

A seemingly half-interested Kelly stated: "I'm going to lay down," as she lied back on the floor.

Jan openly admitted: "This isn't going to work," as the three friends and colleagues started the meditation session.

Albert yelled: "Be quiet!" as the pair's snickers transformed into uncontrolled guffaws.

MORNING MAYHEM

Last week, Kelly insisted "I know I'm on camera!" to a Live producer as she admitted she "doesn't know what to do" during an unusual show moment.

Kelly and Mark Consuelos, also 52, were joined by Maxine the Fluffy Corgi and her owner Bryan Reisburg who were in the studio to teach the hosts how to create a viral video.

To create the eye-catching moment, Bryan placed the corgi in a backpack, strapped the pack onto Mark's back, had both the host and the dog put on sunglasses, and pointed a fan, blowing confetti, at their faces.

Bryan announced: "If it feels stupid you're doing something right," as he recorded Mark.

Kelly stepped aside as her husband added to the madness by mounting a scooter with Maxine still on his back.

She looked overwhelmed as she watched the confusion happening on stage.

The host then called out to the show's producer: "I know I'm on camera Gelman but I don't know what to do with my hands," as she waved them back and forth.

After the video was recorded Bryan admitted: "Frequently when you're filming with a dog, like right now, it's going to be a mess but you get what you get, and you post it, and you hope for something good."

Mark asked the audience: "Are we going viral?" prompting the audience to cheer in response.
