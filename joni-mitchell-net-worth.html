JONI Mitchell is a singer and songwriter with a decades-long career.

Joni has accumulated several awards throughout the years and earned her spot in multiple Halls of Fame.

What is Joni Mitchell's net worth?

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Joni Mitchell has an estimated net worth of around $150million.

As a college student, Joni worked as a performer at The Depression Coffee Shop and sang on local television and radio shows.

She moved from rural Canada to Ontario at the age of 20, where she began pursuing a full-time music career.

Joni performed folk music at various small gatherings and venues as she started off.

Joni signed with the record label Reprise in 1968, the same year she released her debut album: Song to a Seagull.

Her next album came out the following year, and Joni was awarded her first GRAMMY for Best Folk Performance.

The 78-year-old musician has gone on to release a total of 17 studio albums.

How many GRAMMYs does Joni Mitchell have?

Joni Mitchell has been an active musician since the 1960s and has been awarded a multitude of praises throughout her career.

She earned her first GRAMMY in 1969, followed by eight more.

In total, Joni has been nominated for 17 GRAMMY Awards, winning:

  • 1969: Best Folk Performance
  • 1974: Best Arrangement Accompanying Vocalist(s)
  • 1995: Best Pop Album and Best Album Package
  • 2000: Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album
  • 2002: Lifetime Achievement Award
  • 2008: Album of the Year and Best Pop Instrumental Performance
  • 2016: Best Album Notes

She was inducted into the Canadian Music Hall of Fame in 1981.

In 1997, Joni was also inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Who is Joni Mitchell?

Born on November 7, 1943, Joni Mitchell hails from Alberta, Canada.

While Joni's mother was a school teacher, her father worked for the Royal Canadian Air Force as a flight lieutenant responsible for instructing new pilots out of Fort Macleod.

Following World War II, her father worked as a local grocer.

Joni was diagnosed with polio at nine and spent weeks in the hospital. Although the disease weakened her hands, Joni taught herself the ukulele and guitar.

Joni graduated from Aden Bowman Collegiate and attended art classes at the Saskatoon Technical Collegiate under expressionist painter Henry Bonli.

She also spent time at the Alberta College of Art during the 1960s.

In February of 1965, Joni gave birth to Kelly Dale Anderson, who she put up for adoption. The two were reunited for the first time over 30 years later, in 1997.
