Inside The Bold and the Beautiful star Scott Clifton's wild 'real soap opera life

 Posted on May 14, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  659 words  |  Aldo Pusey

THE Bold and the Beautiful star Scott Clifton has revealed the way in which his real life is a lot like what fans see on soap operas.

The 37-year-old has shared the story of his grandfather, who he claimed “was a legitimate con artist.”

In the October 17 issue of Soap Opera Digest, Scott, along with some fellow soap opera stars, were asked about the “soapiest thing” that ever happened to them in their lives.

A few actors detailed moments from their love lives or personal injuries, but Scott’s response stood out and made it to the very top of the magazine’s list.

The Bold and the Beautiful star, who plays Liam on the soap, opened up about his grandfather (his dad Ron Snyder's dad), who one day just “disappeared.” 

Scott told the outlet: “When my dad was, like, nine years old, his father, who was a traveling salesman, just disappeared one day and never came home again.

“Nobody knew what happened.”

Eventually, Scott’s dad decided to hire a detective to look into his own dad, to find out what happened to him and where he’d disappeared to all those years ago.

The investigation led to the Bold actor and his father discovering that “this man was known across the country as ‘Jake the Fake.’”

The 37-year-old continued: “He was a legitimate con artist, and he would marry someone, have kids, start a family, do his con and when the walls started closing in, he would skip town, start a new family and [assume] a new identity.”

“That’s pretty soapy,” Scott insisted, wrapping up his wild personal tale.

The father-of-one, who shares son Ford with his wife-of-10-years Nicole, knows a thing or two about soaps, having been part of that world for most of his career.

He currently plays Liam Spencer on The Bold and the Beautiful – a role he’s been in since 2010.

Prior to that, Scott starred as Schuyler Joplin on One Life to Live for a couple of years.

In 2003, he joined General Hospital in the role of Dillon Quartermaine, and he remained on the show until 2007.

Before landing his GH gig, he’d had guest roles on shows like Roswell and Judging Amy.

Scott wasn’t the only actor to dish on the soapiest aspects of their off-screen lives.

Days of Our Lives actress Cady McClain, who’s been starring as Jennifer Horton since 2020, dished about the time she was rushed to the hospital while on set.

“I was hit with a kidney infection while I was at work [as All My Children’s Dixie], and they had to have an ambulance come in and take me out of there,” she revealed.

The 53-year-old went on: “It just felt like, ‘How much soapier can you get? I’m on a soap and then you have to have an ambulance come and roll me out on a gurney.’ 

“It was pretty, pretty ridiculous. I was really embarrassed.”

General Hospital star Kathleen Gati (Dr. Liesl Obrecht) also had an embarrassing personal story to share.

She shared with the magazine: “About 30 years ago, I was living in Manhattan on the fifth floor of a walk-up building. My neighbor called me frantically to say there were people in my apartment. 

“I immediately called the police. The place was a disaster — clothes everywhere, drawers opened and ransacked. But we found no sign of a forced entry.” 

After looking around for a while with the cops, nothing appeared to be missing.
