Ada Hegerberg Absence From The Norway Women Football Team

After a five-year absence, Norwegian soccer star Ada Hegerberg’s return to the national team has sparked hope for success at Euro 2022.

In 2017, Hegerberg took the decision to step away from soccer, driven by her frustration over the lack of support for women’s soccer in Norway.

Still, changes within the soccer association and personal reflections have brought her back.

Hegerberg’s comeback has been impressive, scoring a hat-trick in her first match against Kosovo.

She joins forces with Barcelona’s Caroline Graham Hansen, who stepped up in her absence, forming a solid attacking duo for Norway.

The team boasts other talented players like Guro Reiten and Ingrid Syrstad Engen in midfield.

However, despite the star power, Norway has struggled with team cohesion and balance on the pitch.

Critics have targeted their system and style of play, as the lack of collective performance actively hinders their success.

As Euro 2022 approaches, there is hope that the team can find the right balance and make the most of their talented squad.

Likewise, the federation’s efforts to improve conditions for women’s Soccer in Norway have been significant, reflecting a positive change driven partly by Hegerberg’s initial stance.

Regardless of the outcome of Euro 2022, Hegerberg’s return has already impacted the women’s game in Norway.

Her First Appearance After A Long Time

In March 2022, Ada Hegerberg ended her five-year absence from the Norwegian national team and made an impressive comeback.

Moreover, in her first game in April, she scored a hat-trick, leading Norway to a 5–1 victory against Kosovo in the 2023 FIFA Women’s World Cup qualification.

Due to her outstanding performance, Norway rightfully included Hegerberg in their 23-player squad for the FIFA Women’s World Cup 2023.

Her return to the team and selection for the tournament marked a significant moment in her career, and fans eagerly anticipated her contributions on the global stage.
