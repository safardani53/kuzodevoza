I think the 20-1 about this novice chaser looks a mad price for Cheltenham Festival success

 Posted on April 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  381 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

CHELTENHAM got the jumps action off to a fine start the weekend before last and one horse who caught the eye was promising Encanto Bruno.

I’m sure shrewd Gavin Cromwell already has the Coral Cup as his big target here in March.

Cromwell has worked wonders at Prestbury Park with dual Stayers' Hurdle hero Flooring Porter one horse who immediately jumps to mind.

The trainer showed his versatility by saddling him to a debut novice chase success over the same track too.

He's very clever with his placings so always make sure you have a Cromwell horse or two in your tracker.

FESTIVAL PUNT
I’M not convinced about favourite Gaelic Warrior and I prefer the chances of STAY AWAY FAY in the Brown Advisory Novices’ Chase.

Paul Nicholls’ star is a staying machine and proved himself on this track when winning the Albert Bartlett at the Festival last year.

I know 10-1 is not big but I rate him highly — so let’s have £30 of Betfair’s dosh on him to win.

I also want to cover him with another Festival hero IROKO who is totally unexposed as a stayer. A tenner each-way at 20-1 please.
