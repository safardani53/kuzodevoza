FA investigating incident at Derby vs Cardiff after Pride Park evacuated over flares set off in the

 Posted on May 25, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  197 words  |  Martina Birk

THE Football Association have revealed they are investigating an incident at Derby, which led to the stadium being evacuated.

SunSport approached The FA and they revealed they were probing the incident, reportedly sparked by flares set off by fans of opponents Cardiff in the away end.

It is believed to be the first time flares have caused a whole stadium to be evacuated in England.

It meant those already in the stadium had to leave the ground before kick-off in the Championship match.
Supporters were left stunned when the public announcer told everyone to leave.

Video footage showed players and staff from both teams sprinting off the pitch as the fire exits were opened.

The message said: "Attention please, this is an emergency. Please leave the stadium by the nearest available exit."

There were also reports of fans without tickets walking back in through the fire exits as paying punters made their way back in.

A crowd of 25,873 filed back in and the kick-off time wasn't affected.

The match finished in a 1-1 draw.
