Divorce of Chris Moore from Carol Lum

Chris divorced Carol Lum while his daughter Carissa was just 10 years old. It was a difficult period for their daughter at the time. She was suffering with her confidence and a body problem at the moment. Carissa said that she did not surf as a child. She also said that before her parents separated, her father would push her into the waves, but her mother would grab her.

She didn’t surf with her mother since she was affiliated with surfing with her father. She is grateful to her mother for teaching her to fight for what she wants. She loves her father the most since he has played such an important part in her life. He has always been really supportive of her. Her parents remained close to her despite their divorce.

Relationship situation

Carissa Moore is married to Luke Untermann, the co-founder of Banan, and her lover and spouse. They married on the grounds of the North Shore Family estate, which belonged to one of their Punahou classmates. She was pleased that things had turned out this way. They are really thankful to one another. Carissa’s younger sister, Cayla Moore, served as maid of honor at their wedding.

They’ve been married for four years, having exchanged wedding vows on December 16, 2017. Kelia Termini, Stephanie Gilmore, Bethany Hamilton, John Florence, and Coco Ho were among the elite surfers and bride’s peers that attended the wedding.

Carissa and Luke Untermann met via a mutual friend

Carissa and Luke Untermann met in high school at Punahou High School, where Barak Obama also attended. Carissa and Luke met in high school and fell in love. Carissa was the Punahou High School’s public personality, and she was incredibly bright and enthusiastic. Every student at the institution admired her since she was also quite skilled at surfing and anchoring.

Carissa Moore’s bio

Carissa is a four-time world-champion American professional surfer from Honolulu, Hawaii. Glamour Magazine named her Woman of the Year in 2013. Carol Lum, her mother, and Chris Moore, her father, gave birth to her. She was born on August 27, 1992, in Honolulu, Hawaii, United States.

Nike, Red Bull, Target, Hurley, Subaru Hawaii, and many more companies support her. She has won the ASP Women’s World Tour four times. National Geographic also awarded her “Adventurer of the Year.” Cayla Moore, her younger sister, raised her. She represented the United States in the 2020 Olympic Games in Tokyo.
