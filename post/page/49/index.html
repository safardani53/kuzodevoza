Guru Gobind Singh, born Gobind Rai, is the tenth guru of Sikhism, a spiritual master, warrior, poet and philosopher. When his father, Guru Tegh Bahadur, was executed by Aurangzeb, Guru Gobind Singh was officially appointed Sikh leader at the age of nine, becoming the tenth and last human Sikh Guru. His four sons died during his lifetime - two in battle, two by the Mughal army. Among his notable contributions to Sikhism were the establishment of the Sikh militant community known as the Khalsa in 1699 and the birth of the five Ks, five creeds that Sikh Khalsa followers always wear.
