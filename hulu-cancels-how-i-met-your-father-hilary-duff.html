HULU has canceled How I Met Your Father after airing just two seasons of the popular spinoff series.

The show served as a follow-up to How I Met Your Mother, and featured cameos by original cast members Cobie Smulders and Neil Patrick Harris.

Hulu confirmed that How I Met Your Father would not return for a third season on Friday, according to Deadline.

The show was helmed by Hilary Duff and starred Kim Cattrall, Chris Lowell, Francia Raisa, Tom Ainsley, and Tien Tran.

It was written by Isaac Aptaker and Elizabeth Berger and built upon the story developed on How I Met Your Mother.

The pair served as executive producers on the show alongside How I Met Your Mother creators Carter Bays, Craig Thomas, and the original series director and executive producer Pam Fryman.

The show debuted in 2022 – eight years after the last episode of How I Met Your Mother aired.

None of the original cast was featured as a series regular on the spinoff series.

Some of the cast of How I Met Your Father lived in the same apartment occupied by Josh Radnor (Ted) and Jason Segel (Marshall) on How I Met Your Mother, however.

SEASON TWO

How I Met Your Father returned for a second season in January, wrapping up in July.

The final episode – titled Okay Fine, It's a Hurricane – aired on July 11.

During its short run on TV, the show was nominated for five Emmy Awards.

It was nominated for Outstanding Production Design for a Narrative Program; Outstanding Cinematography For a Series; Outstanding Picture Editing for a Multi-Camera Comedy Series; Outstanding Cinematography For a Multi-Camera Series; and Outstanding Multi-Camera Picture Editing For a Comedy Series.

It won the latter awards – both in 2022.
