Before we dive into her career, let’s learn more about her father.

DeCovan Kadell “Dee” Brown was a professional basketball player for 12 seasons in the NBA, playing for the Boston Celtics, Toronto Raptors, and Orlando Magic.

The Boston Celtics drafted him as the 19th pick in the 1990 NBA draft from Jacksonville University.

He won the NBA Slam Dunk Contest in 1991 and was an NBA All-Rookie Team member in his debut season.

Brown established EDGE Basketball, LLC in 2005, training players from middle school to professional ranks, and later became a coach in the WNBA, NBA Development League, and the NBA.

He is currently the general manager of the Los Angeles Clippers NBA G League team.

Dee Brown Daughter Lexie Brown: Age Gap And Family Tree

Alexis Kiah Brown, also known as Lexie, was born in Boston, Massachusetts, on October 27, 1994, to her parents, Dee and Tammy Brown.

Like her father, Lexie also has made her name for herself. She is an American professional basketball player currently playing for the Los Angeles Sparks in the Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA).

Brown’s career started as the ninth overall pick in the 2018 WNBA draft when she was selected by the Connecticut Sun.

After a year, she moved to the Minnesota Lynx, where she played before joining her current team.

Before her professional career, Brown played for the University of Maryland and later transferred to Duke University.

During her freshman year at Maryland, she helped lead the team to the NCAA tournament’s Final Four and earned the nickname “Big Shot Brown.”

Brown also received accolades like the ACC All-Freshmen Team and ACC All-Academic Team.

After her sophomore year, she transferred to Duke University and contributed significantly to Duke’s success during her junior year.
