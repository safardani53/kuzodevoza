Who is Rachel Zegler?

Rachel Zegler is an actress, singer, and songwriter, raised in New Jersey by her American father and Colombian mother.

Having been one of 30,000 people to audition, she was selected to play the role of Maria in Steven Spielberg’s remake of the classic West Side Story (2021), opposite Ansel Elgort as Tony.

She is the youngest artist to win the Golden Globe awards. Apart from it, she is the first artist of Polish descent to win this award.

In the year 2023, she was listed by Forbes in the list titled “30 under 30”. She is also very popular on social media.

On YouTube, she has more than 218K subscribers, which is increasing exponentially.

Rachel Zegler Age

Rachel Zegler is 22 years old as of 2023, born on May 3, 2001. At the age of 22, Rachel has already established herself as a notable stage actress, showcasing her talent and passion for the performing arts.

Early Life of Rachel Zegler

Born on 03 May 2001, Rachel Zegler’s age is 22 years as of 2023. She was born and raised in a well-settled Christian family in Hackensack, New Jersey, the United States.

Zegler is an American by nationality and has a belief in the Christian religion. She completed her early schooling at Immaculate Conception High School, in New Jersey, United States.

After that, she enrolled herself at a Local College from where she completed her graduation. From the very beginning of her childhood, she was more interested in acting, singing, and various co-curricular activities rather than in studying.

Zegler’s parents are Gina and Craig Zegler. She was named after the fictional character Rachel Green from the TV show ‘Friends’.

She has an older sister. Her mother is of Colombian descent. Zegler’s maternal grandmother immigrated from Colombia to the United States in the 1960s.

Her father is of Polish descent.

Rachel Zegler Career

Zegler began her career starring in several musicals while attending Immaculate Conception High School.

It included playing Belle in Beauty and the Beast (2016), Ariel in The Little Mermaid (2017), Dorothy Brock in 42nd Street (2018), and Princess Fiona in Shrek the Musical (2019).

For all four performances, she received Metro Award nominations in the Actress in a Leading Role category.

Her other roles include Serena in Legally Blonde, Cosette in Les Misérables, and Millie in Thoroughly Modern Millie.

Zegler’s YouTube channel has been active since July 2015 and has also received attention. As of 2020, Zegler’s video of herself singing “Shallow”, from A Star Is Born, has garnered over 11 million views on Twitter.

Debut into the movies and glory

In January 2018, director Steven Spielberg posted an open casting call via Twitter for a new film adaptation of West Side Story.

Zegler, then 16 years old, responded to the casting call tweets with videos of herself singing “Tonight” and “I Feel Pretty”.

She performed the roles at the Bergen Performing Arts Center in 2017.

On June 22, 2021, she was cast to play the character of Snow White in Disney’s Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs. Zegler was selected from over 30,000 applicants for the role of Maria, marking her film debut.

The film was released in December 2021 and earned acclaim from critics and audiences.

 For her performance, she received a Critics’ Choice Movie Award Nomination for Best Young Performer and Won the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical.

It makes her the first actress of Colombian descent to win in that category as well as the youngest winner in that category at 20 years old. She has since been cast in films such as DC’s Shazam: Fury of the Gods, Disney’s Snow White, and Spellbound.

On May 31, 2022, Variety reported that Zegler had been cast in the role of Lucy Gray Baird after Zegler teased it on her Twitter the night before.

Zegler reported receiving death threats following her casting.

She is also scheduled to join the cast of the film Shazam! Fury of the Gods. The film is scheduled for release in 2023.

Rachel has also appeared in The George Lucas Talk Show in the episode titled Adam Pally and Rachel Zegler.
