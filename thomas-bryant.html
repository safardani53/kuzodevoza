Bryant spent much of his time playing basketball with buddies from various schools, and he rarely went home.

He used to ride the bus to the MCA and play basketball with his teammates.

From game to game, he learned from his mistakes and attempted to improve.

Thomas Bryant | High School Career

Bryant joined the Bishop Kearney High school located in Irondequoit, New York, to complete his early education.

Moreover, he played basketball as a sophomore and helped his team win a New York Class AA Tournament and New York Federation Tournament of Championship.

He attended Huntington Prep school, later located in West Virginia, and completed further schooling following his sophomore year.

Similarly, as a junior in high school, he notched 13.9 points, 12.9 rebounds, and 2.8 blocks in each game.

Bryant was a member of the AAU team when he was in junior year. He had a victory against Whitney Young with 23 points and 12 rebounds on January 3, 2015.

Furthermore, Bryant’s senior year was a great one for him, as ESPN ranked him as the 20th top recruit in the 2015 class.

As a result, he was extensively approached by several schools.

Finally, he chose Indiana over Syracuse since the basketball program there was in disarray and went against his mother’s wishes.

During Bryant’s decision-making process, Syracuse was involved in an NCAA investigation that resulted in the loss of 12 scholarships over the following four years.

On April 4, 2015, at the Dick’s Sporting Goods National Tournament on ESPN, Bryant made his final verbal commitment to Indiana.

Bryant scored 9 points and collected three rebounds in 16 minutes for the East team at the McDonald’s All-American Game.

Furthermore, Tom Crean recruited him for the sixth straight year as a McDonald’s All-American.

Thomas Bryant | College Career

Thomas’s decision to join Indiana made him play there for three years, from 2015-to 17. 

During his play against Creighton, he had a break-out performance in an 86-65 victory. He notched 17 points, seven rebounds, and four blocks in Hoosiers victory.

Similarly, Bryant wins against Kentucky in the NCAA Tournament with 19 points and five rebounds.

At the season end, Bryant was honored with the named both Big Ten All-Freshman team and Third-Team All-Big Ten.

Bryant announced his sophomore season, and while his shooting % dropped, he still amassed 12.6 points, 6.6 rebounds, and 1.5 blocks per game.

Thomas Bryant | Professional Career

Los Angeles Lakers

Thomas started his first step into his NBA Career in 2007. The Utah Jazz selected Bryant 42nd overall in the 2017 NBA draft on June 22, 2017.

Later, he was dealt with the Los Angeles Lakers alongside Josh Hart, the 30th selection, for Tony Bradley, the Lakers’ 28th pick.

Bryant signed his rookie scale deal with the Lakers on July 30, 2017, and the Lakers waived him on June 30, 2018.

Moreover, Thomas Bryant was assigned to the South Bay Lakers, Los Angeles Lakers G League club.

Bryant completed his 2017-18 seasons with the Lakers, and during his seasons, he was named to All-NBA G League First Team.

Washington Wizards

On July 2nd, 2018, Bryant found a new home, and the Washington Wizards picked up Bryant off waivers.

Similarly, Bryant scored 31 points on December 22, 2018, against the Phoenix Suns, shooting 14–14 from the field. He tied for the fourth-most field goals made without a miss in NBA history.

Unfortunately, Bryant tested positive for Covid-19 on July 9, 2020, during the 2020 pandemic.

During his game against the Miami Heat in 2021, Thomas suffered extreme pain in his left knee,  ripping the anterior cruciate ligament.
