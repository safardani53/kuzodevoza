I Masturbate to Ryan Gosling, and IDGAF!

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  374 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Anna Kendrick is the queen of social media, if not life as we know it. A quick glance at her feed is perhaps the greatest thing you’ll read on any given day.

Especially if she talks about pleasing herself to Ryan Gosling films.

This would be true if she were an old friend of yours, or even a random person, but the fact that it’s Academy Award nominee Anna Kendrick makes it even better.

Her hilariously unfiltered tweets totally make her the one person you would take to that desert island with you, or follow on Twitter if you had to pick just one.

Perhaps her most famous Tweet was this one from two years ago:

“Ugh – NEVER going to a Ryan Gosling movie in a theater again. Apparently masturbating in the back row is still considered ‘inappropriate.'”

Well, yeah. We’ve all been there at some point … right ladies?!

Most people who Tweet something like that quickly take it down, but Anna has not done so, and luckily for us there’s no sign of her censoring herself …

Nylon’s February cover girl told the magazine frankly that she doesn’t care what people think. At all: “I don’t give a f–k. Twitter is only 140 characters.”

“There’s really not that much that I could reveal, then regret later.”

As for how Gosling personally would react to her infamous comment?

“Thank God I’ve never been in a room with him!” Kendrick laughs.

“I don’t feel embarrassed, though. I’m sure he has a sense of humor.”

“But I’d probably feel like I would have to address [the tweet],” Anna muses to Nylon, “and I’d end up saying something to make it much, much worse.”

If she can’t filter herself in 140 characters or less and while typing out her comments onto a screen, just imagine how delightfully unfiltered she is in person.

This wasn’t the first self-pleasure comment she made, either.

“Every time I talk to a fancy journalist,” she mused last fall, “and they ask what I do in my free time my scumbag brain goes ‘say masturbate, it’ll be hilarious.'”

Keep fighting the good fight, Anna. And you too, Ryan.
