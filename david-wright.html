David Wright Biography

David Allen Wright was born on December 20, 1982, in Norfolk, Virginia, to parents Rhon and Elisa Wright. Rhon, his father, was a police officer with the Norfolk Police Department. David spent his childhood in Virginia with his three other brothers. While in elementary school, he learned and practiced alongside future Major League player Michael Cuddyer, and in high school, he played on teams with future stars such as Ryan Zimmerman, Mark Reynolds, B.J. Upton, and Justin Upton. Later, when he enrolled at Georgia Tech, he continued to pursue his passion by playing baseball for the college team. David had a batting average of 0.538 in high school.

Career

After the Mets lost Mike Hampton in free agency to the Colorado Rockies in 2001, David Wright was drafted in the supplemental round. On July 21, 2004, David made his major league debut at third base against the Montreal Expos. David finished his first season with a 0.293 batting average, 14 home runs, and 40 RBI in 263 at-bats across 69 games. He was chosen as the Rookie of the Year.

On November 30, 2012, Ed Coleman and WFAN reported that David’s contract with the Mets had been extended. David agreed to a seven-year, $138 million contract extension with the Mets. After David completed the physical, the agreement became official on December 4. He was sidelined for 15 days in April 2015 due to a strained right hamstring. He was given a 60-day rehabilitation period. He then regained his fitness and resumed his career.
